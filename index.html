Why are we doing this?

16% of UK adults are functionally illiterate, and much of that comes from a lack of access to books. A third of households have fewer than 20 books while library loans have almost halved in a decade. Low literacy costs the UK £81bn a year.

In 2001, then Secretary for Education David Blunkett lamented that “As many as seven million adults of working age in England cannot read or write as well as an eleven year old.” That number has not changed. 18 years later, fewer 11-year-olds reach the expected result in reading and maths. Another generation of adults will grow up to be less literate than before, in an age where literacy is even more essential.

When you go to a cafe that has a trove, you’re enabling us to put troves in schools, hospitals and food banks - and grabbing a good read. Read further.
